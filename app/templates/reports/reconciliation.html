About daily-level variances

Amber rows are days where our pre-refund total disagrees with TikTok's GMV by more than a cent. Variance is shown as ours − TikTok: positive means we're counting more than TikTok on that day, negative means TikTok is counting more.

These small daily gaps are expected. TikTok Seller Center buckets orders by their own reporting timezone, while our app keeps each order's exact placed_at timestamp from the orders CSV. Orders placed near midnight at the boundary land on different calendar days in the two systems — a single order can flip Day X's variance by its full net amount, which is almost always offset by an equal-and-opposite flip on the neighboring day.

Month totals reconcile cleanly. Boundary-crossing within a month nets to zero, so the only true gap that matters is at the very first/last day of the period. For March 2026 the net monthly residual was $7.80 — well under typical finance-team tolerance and not worth a fix that would risk other downstream joins.

Investigated 2026-05-19: the empirical offset between the two systems is exactly 1 hour. We deliberately do NOT shift our stored timestamps because the gap is immaterial at the monthly level and changing the data risks breaking settlement reconciliation, P&L date filters, and policy-violation flagging. Treat amber rows as informational, not as bugs.
