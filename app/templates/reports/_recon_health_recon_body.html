About daily-level variances

Amber rows are days where our GMV disagrees with TikTok's reported GMV by more than a cent. Variance is shown as ours − TikTok: positive means we're counting more than TikTok on that day, negative means TikTok is counting more.

Settled months tie to $0.00 per day. We bucket each order by its shop-local (Seller Center) calendar day — the same DST-aware reporting zone TikTok uses — so once a month is fully settled, every day matches TikTok to the cent (Feb–May 2026 each reconcile with zero off-days). This replaced an earlier model that left a ~1-hour midnight-boundary artifact; that's resolved.

Amber on a settled (older) day is a real break worth investigating — typically a missing or partial import, or a TikTok-side restatement — not timezone noise. The dashboard surfaces these as reconciliation-break alerts once a day is past its settling grace window.

Amber on the most recent days is expected and clears on its own. TikTok's Shop Analytics aggregate lags its own order ledger by a few days, so the current and last several days can under-report until TikTok finalizes them — that's provisional data settling, not an error. Let those days age out of the grace window before treating a gap as a break.
