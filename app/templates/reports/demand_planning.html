How is this calculated? (plain English)

For every SKU we look at three things: how fast it sells, how much we have on hand right now, and how long it takes to restock. Then we work out whether we need to place a PO today, how many units to buy, and when to expect a stockout.

How fast it sells (velocity)

We count units shipped over the last 60 days, divide by 60, and call that the daily sales rate. We also compute a 14-day rate as a "recent trend" signal. Bundles count as demand for each component inside them (a "Kit A" sale of 2 means 2 of each component inside the kit). Cancelled/withdrawn orders don't count — only Shipped + Completed. Free samples don't count either — they're fulfilled from a separate TikTok seeding inventory pool that doesn't touch our sellable stock.

One quirk: if a single day had a viral spike (e.g. a creator went viral and 500 units sold), that day would inflate the 60-day average for two months and trick us into over-buying. So we cap each day at the larger of 3× the typical day or 5× the overall average — outliers get clipped, normal good days don't. The 14-day number you see in the table is uncapped on purpose, so the buyer can spot a trend changing.

Days of supply & Stockout date

Days of supply = on-hand ÷ daily velocity. Stockout = today + days of supply. So "Stockout: Jun 12" means at the current sales rate, we'll run out around June 12 if no new inventory arrives.

Reorder point

The on-hand level at which a PO must be placed to avoid a stockout before it arrives. Formula: (velocity × lead time) + safety stock, where safety stock = z × σ × √lead_time for any SKU with non-zero daily-demand variation.

z comes from the page-level Service level dropdown (90% → z=1.28, 95% → z=1.65, 97.5% → z=1.96). Higher service level = bigger buffer = fewer stockouts. σ is the standard deviation of the trailing 60-day daily demand series (uncapped — using the spike-dampened series would under-buffer the very volatility we're insuring against). SKUs with σ=0 (no recent sales) fall back to a flat-percentage buffer, but the bulk of the catalog uses the variance formula.

Suggested order quantity

How many units to actually buy when we trip the reorder point. We aim to cover lead time + cover days of forward demand (default 14 + 45 = 59 days), minus whatever's already on hand or in transit.

Formula: velocity × (lead time + cover days) − on hand − in-transit. Then we round up to the supplier's MOQ if set, and to the next case-pack multiple.

Status colors

  • Out of stock — on hand ≤ 0.
  • At risk — will run out before a new PO can arrive (days of supply < lead time).
  • Reorder now — still has runway, but below reorder point — place a PO today.
  • Healthy — sitting between reorder point and overstock line.
  • Overstocked — more than 180 days of supply on hand (capital sitting idle).
  • Discontinued / No velocity — flagged not-reorderable, or never sold in the 60-day window.

Investment outlook (top tiles)

Projected capital required to keep selling at the current rate for the next 30 / 60 / 90 days, ignoring SKUs that are already healthy or overstocked. Reorder Now = the immediate PO total for SKUs currently below their reorder point.

Defaults: lead time 14 days, service level {{ (v.service_level * 100) | round(1) }}%, cover {{ v.cover_days }} days. Lead time is tweakable per SKU on the drill-down page; the page-wide service-level and cover dropdowns above let you run what-ifs across the catalog.
