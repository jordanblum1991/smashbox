Understanding the columns

TikTok Ads Manager splits every dollar of reported spend into three buckets, depending on where the money came from. The Total column is the sum of the three — that's the figure TikTok considers your "ad spend" and the figure that flows into the P&L as TikTok Ads (GMV Max).

Cash — pay-as-you-go billing
Charges billed directly to your linked payment method (credit card / bank) as the campaign runs. This bucket is only populated when you're on TikTok's pay-as-you-go billing model — every few days TikTok rolls up the spend and bills it to your card. For Smashbox this is $0 because the account is funded via a pre-loaded balance instead (see Credit below).
Credit — pre-funded balance drawdown
Drawdown from money you already paid TikTok by topping up your ad account balance (e.g. wiring $10K up front to fund a flight of campaigns). TikTok holds that money as a "credit balance" and debits it as ads serve. This is real cash spend — the cash just left your account earlier, when you funded the balance, rather than at the time of each ad impression. All of Smashbox's reported spend lands here because the account is pre-funded.
Ad Credits — TikTok-issued promotional credits
Promotional credits TikTok grants you (welcome offers, performance incentives, account-manager comps, make-goods for platform issues, etc.). This is the only bucket that is NOT a real cash cost — TikTok still attributes the spend to your account but no money changed hands. When this column shows a number, your true out-of-pocket cost for that month is Total − Ad Credits.

Why the P&L uses Total, not (Total − Ad Credits): The P&L follows TikTok's reporting convention so the line ties directly to Ads Manager and to any reconciliation with TikTok's invoices. If we ever want a "true cash-out" view of marketing spend, the formula is Total − Ad Credits — that figure appears in the Ad Credits Used KPI tile above for quick reference.
